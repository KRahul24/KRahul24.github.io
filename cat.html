    • Common Admission Test (CAT)

    Common Admission Test (CAT) is national-level management entrance examination conducted by the Indian Institutes of Management (IIM). Apart from 20 IIMs, CAT scores are used for admission to various management institutes across the country. CAT 2017 was conducted sucessfully on November 26, 2017 (Sunday). The exam was conducted by IIM Lucknow.
    CAT 2018 is expected to be held in November-December, registration for the same will begin in July.  The CAT 2017 results were announced on January 8, 2018. 20 candidates scored 100 percentile yet again. The candidates can download their scorecards from the official website. Admission process through CAT at IIMs and other B-Schools is currently underway. This year, the CAT question paper and answer keys were released for the first time and the candidates were allowed to raise objection to answers they found incorrect.  
    Common Admission Test or CAT is the major and most popular MBA entrance exam for admission in 20 IIMs and over 100 B-schools across India. Conducted by IIMs on a rotational basis, CAT undergoes major changes or introduction of new elements every year. CAT 2018 is expected to be notified by IIMs in the last Sunday of July 2018. It is likely that CAT 2018 will be held in the last week of November.
    The CAT 2018 registration process is scheduled to commence from the first week of August 2018. Around two lakh candidates apply and appear for the computer-based test every year. Last year has been marked as one of the milestones for CAT owing to the release of official question paper and answer key.
    IIM Calcutta and IIM Kozhikode are the biggest contenders to convene CAT 2018, although any of the other top four IIMs can be assigned this responsibility. During the last 5 years, only these two IIMs have not convened the Common Admission Test (CAT).
